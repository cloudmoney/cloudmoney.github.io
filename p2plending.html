How to P2P

The idea behind p2p platforms are to allow individuals to maintain an economy while having a community of like-minded individuals to share information or assets, making contractual relationships without any officially approved third-party overseeing the terms.

As a result, the primary feature focus at the moment is on lending/borrowing funds.

Since a p2p site is running on decentralized currency, everyone can make financial agreements with everyone and earn the interests which both parties agree upon. Your p2p account should be treated as a bank account, with a strong password and common sense. Nothing is guaranteed and you might lose some, or all, of your investments.

Read the FAQ/ToS of respective site before participating.

Tips and Tricks

Guidelines for p2p lending



Always research the borrower, google is your friend. Sometimes a quick google search reveals stolen accounts or repeated scam attempts. Be wary, always! At the very least, check other p2p sites for account name/listing name to see if a negative profile comes up.

Dont keep all your eggs in one basket, diversify your portofolio (investments) to reduce the risk of losing your money on a single bad borrower.

If you are borrowing money, make sure you are aware what APR (annual percentage rate) you are paying. The measuring unit of interest varies from site to site, so be careful not to get confused in monthly/daily/yearly interest rates.

It is always a good thing to be social with other users when you get the chance, asking questions or maintaining conversations builds trust, even though you might not realize it. The more your presence is known, the more likely you are to receive funding.

I personally would like to recommend btcpop.co for the innovative IPO feature and (soon-to-come) collateral-based loans.

IPO's allow you to invest your bitcoin in start-up companies and earn dividends paid out to shareholders based on the amount of shares you hold. Anyone can submit an IPO, but it needs to be approved by pop staff.

Collateral-based loans allow you to take out a loan without any ID verification, you just provide enough collateral to support your loan so in case you do not pay back your loan, the collateral can be sold off to cover it. Should the loan be paid off as agreed with lender, the collateral are returned to borrower in full.

Example:You wanna take a loan of 1 BTC, paying back 1.2 BTC a week later. Collateral would have to be more than 1.2 BTC (provided before loan funds) to get the loan.

P2P sites

Perhaps the most interesting use of bitcoin and crypto currencies to date is p2p lending. Through user-friendly interfaces people from all over the world are brought together in a economically focused environment. Manage your own personal finances like a professional bank, and enjoy the many, many benefits of decentralized currency.

The two most popular platforms are btcpop.co and btcjam.com , however there are many more.

When you have registered an account at either site you are ready to start investing your coins and earn interest. In order to take out a loan, you will need to verify your official documents.

P2p sites are a great way to secure your bitcoins for longer periods, while also having the opportunities to keep them active (earning interest).

If you are the type of person who values privacy over everything, i recommend you make an account over at bitcointalk.org. When you are there, go to Marketplace > Lending and look for a lender or make a thread asking for a loan.
