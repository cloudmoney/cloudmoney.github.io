What is a Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a form of digital currency, created and held electronically. No one controls it. Bitcoins aren’t printed, like dollars or euros – they’re produced by people, and increasingly businesses, running computers all around the world, using software that solves mathematical problems.

It’s the first example of a growing category of money known as cryptocurrency.

Cryptocurrency? That is not money.

Of course it is! You can buy goods and services with it, and even lend/borrow it to/from others. There are also huge exchanges trading cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ethereum etc. just as commodities like Oil, Wheat, Corn, and even Volvo shares, which you probably know of.

Cryptocurrencies, of all kind, are traded with volumes worth hundreds of thousands of dollars every single day, and is getting more and more presence in the modern markets.
